# Play I - The Litany of Glass

Mira arranges the actors in a half-circle and forbids applause.
If the chamber is listening, she tells them, then the room must sound like an oath
instead of a performance.

Each repetition changes one line. Each changed line produces a detail no one rehearsed:

1. A scratched sigil appears on a prop that had been clean.
2. One performer speaks with a voice not their own.
3. The ending arrives before the middle has been played.

## Staging Notes

- Keep the rhythm devotional, not operatic.
- Treat interruptions as revelations rather than mistakes.
- Let the audience wonder whether the play is documentation or contagion.
